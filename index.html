Deposit Goals

Find out how much you need to save to buy a home in your town before your selected age.

How It Works

For every area listed we went to the HM Land Registry to see:

  • What the house price for first time buyers was in each of the 200 largest cities, towns, and districts.
  • How much house prices had risen in the last 17 years (March 2008 - March 2025).

We then projected this forward to account for what house prices would likely be in each area from 2026 - 2042.

This means that for an 18 year old looking to buy at 35, we are looking at house prices in 2042. For a 25 year old looking to buy at 30, we are looking at house prices in 2030, etc.

We then multiplied each of these numbers by 0.2 to come up with the typical 20% deposit that a first time buyer puts down on their property (this 20% figure comes from a 2024 report from the bank Halifax).

We then divided this number by the number of months that passes between your age and the age you want to buy.

For example, if someone started saving from 18-35, then the number was divided by 204 (17*12). Similarly, if someone started saving from 23-35, then the number was divided by 108 (9*12).

We calculate salary requirement by minusing the deposit from the home value and then dividing that figure by 4.5. 4.5x your salary is the typical amount you can borrow for a first time house buyer mortgage at the time of writing (May 2025).
